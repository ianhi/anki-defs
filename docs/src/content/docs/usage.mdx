## Single word lookup

Type any Bangla word and press Enter. The AI returns a definition, an example sentence with the word used in context, and an English translation.

The card preview shows the extracted data. If the AI picked the wrong dictionary form (lemma), a blue **mismatch badge** appears so you can catch it before adding the card.

## Sentence analysis

Paste a full Bangla sentence. The AI breaks it down word by word, explaining vocabulary and grammar. Each word gets its own card preview.

### Highlighting specific words

If you only want cards for certain words in a sentence, wrap them in `**double asterisks**` before sending. Only those words get card previews.

You can also select text in the input box and press **Ctrl+B** to toggle bold highlighting.

<Aside type="tip">
  Highlighting is useful when you mostly understand a sentence but want to learn two or three new
  words from it.
</Aside>

## Editing card previews

Before adding a card, click the **pencil icon** to edit the word or definition inline.

If the AI picked the wrong dictionary form, click the **refresh icon** to re-lemmatize -- the AI suggests the correct base form.

## Adding and skipping cards

Each card preview has two actions:

- **Add to Anki** -- creates the card in your selected deck immediately. If Anki is not running, the card goes into an offline queue instead.
- **Skip** -- dismisses the card preview without creating a card.

### Duplicate detection

Before adding, anki-defs checks your Anki deck for existing cards with the same word. If a duplicate is found, a warning badge appears on the card preview. You can still add the card if you want.

### Offline queue

If Anki is not running when you add a card, it goes into a pending queue. The cards are saved and can be synced to Anki later when it's available.

## Session cards panel

Click the **cards icon** in the header to open the session sidebar. It shows every card you have added (or queued) during this session.

- **Undo** an added card to remove it from Anki
- **Remove** a queued card to discard it before syncing

## Sync button

When Anki is connected, a **sync icon** appears in the header. Click it to trigger an AnkiConnect sync so cards you just added on your laptop appear on your phone via AnkiWeb.

This is especially useful when [accessing anki-defs from your phone over Tailscale](/anki-defs/tailscale/) -- add cards from your phone, then sync to AnkiWeb so they're available everywhere.

## Left-handed mode

Toggle in Settings. Moves the Add/Skip buttons to the left side of card previews for easier one-handed use on mobile.

## Chat persistence

Your conversation history survives page refreshes. The input box, AI responses, and card previews are all restored when you reload the page.
